# Antonio Carlos Jobim Harmonic Language

Jobim's bossa nova harmony uses jazz vocabulary with Brazilian sensibility.

## Characteristic techniques

**Pedal point**: Sustain a bass note while chords change above it.
Example opening of Garota de Ipanema: static bass while harmony floats.

**Chromatic descending bass lines**: Bass descends by half or whole steps
while the harmony follows. Creates inevitable forward motion.

**Parallel minor/major borrowing**: Freely borrow chords from parallel minor.
In C major: use Fm7, Bb7 (from C minor) for color and surprise.

**Nature of the Girl (Garota de Ipanema) analysis**:
F - G7 - Gm7 - Gb7b5 - Fmaj7 - G7 - Gm7 - Gb7b5
The Gb7b5 is a tritone sub of C7, creating a chromatic approach to Fmaj7.

**Wave (Vou Te Contar) opening**:
Dmaj7 - E7 - Em7 - A7 - Dmaj7
A textbook I - II7 - ii7 - V7 - I with a secondary dominant (E7).

## Why Jobim matters for jazz study
His melodies sit perfectly on the chord tones, making his tunes ideal
for learning to hear the relationship between melody and harmony.
