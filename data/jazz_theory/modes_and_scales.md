# Modes and Scales in Jazz

## Dorian Mode
Used over minor ii chords (Dm7). Formula: W H W W W H W.
Example: D Dorian = D E F G A B C D
Characteristic note: the raised 6th (B natural over Dm7)

## Mixolydian Mode
Used over dominant V7 chords (G7). Formula: W W H W W H W.
Example: G Mixolydian = G A B C D E F G
Characteristic note: the b7 (F natural over G7)

## Lydian Mode
Used over major I chords (Cmaj7). Formula: W W W H W W H.
Example: C Lydian = C D E F# G A B C
Characteristic note: the #4 (F# over Cmaj7) adds brightness.

## Altered Scale (Super Locrian)
Used over altered dominant chords (G7alt). All tensions altered: b9, #9, b5/#11, b13.
Formula: H W H W W W W. Example: G altered = G Ab Bb B Db Eb F G
When to use: resolving to a chord a 5th below (G7alt -> Cmaj7).

## Bebop Scale
Dominant bebop scale adds a chromatic passing tone (natural 7) to Mixolydian.
Example: G bebop dominant = G A B C D E F F# G
The extra note keeps chord tones on downbeats when descending in 8th notes.
