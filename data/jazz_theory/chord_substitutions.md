# Chord Substitutions

## Tritone Substitution
Replace a dominant 7th chord with the dominant 7th a tritone away.
G7 can be replaced by Db7 (tritone = 6 semitones apart).
Why: Db7 and G7 share the same tritone (F and B / Cb and E are enharmonic).
The bass moves by half-step into the resolution chord (Db -> C).

## Backdoor Dominant
Replace a V7 chord with the bVII7 chord.
In C major: replace G7 with Bb7.
Bb7 resolves to Cmaj7 via smooth voice leading. Common in ballads.

## Passing Diminished
Insert a diminished 7th chord between two diatonic chords.
Example: Cmaj7 - C#dim7 - Dm7 - G7
The diminished chord connects by chromatic voice leading.

## Secondary Dominant
Any chord can be preceded by its own V7 (the secondary dominant).
In C major: A7 -> Dm7 (A7 is V7/ii, the dominant of Dm7)
Creates momentary tonicization. Heavily used in Jobim and bebop.
