# ii-V-I Progressions

The ii-V-I is the most common chord progression in jazz. In C major:
Dm7 (ii) - G7 (V) - Cmaj7 (I)

The ii chord (minor 7th) creates tension by moving away from tonic.
The V chord (dominant 7th) creates maximum tension with the tritone
between the 3rd and 7th (B and F in G7). The I chord resolves the tension.

In minor keys (modal minor ii-V-i):
Dm7b5 (iim7b5) - G7b9 (V7b9) - Cm7 (im7)
The half-diminished ii chord and altered dominant signal a minor resolution.

Voice leading: The 3rd of G7 (B) resolves up to the root of Cmaj7 (C).
The 7th of G7 (F) resolves down to the 3rd of Cmaj7 (E).
