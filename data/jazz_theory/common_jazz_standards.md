# Common Jazz Standards by Key and Form

## Autumn Leaves (Les feuilles mortes)
Key: G major / E minor. Form: AABA (32 bars).
Chord progression (A section):
Cm7 - F7 - Bbmaj7 - Ebmaj7 - Am7b5 - D7 - Gm (or Gm7)
Features: ii-V-I in both Bb major and G minor. Classic for beginners.

## All The Things You Are (Kern/Hammerstein)
Key: Ab major. Form: AABA (36 bars, unusual 9-bar bridge).
Opens: Fm7 - Bbm7 - Eb7 - Abmaj7 - Dbmaj7 - G7 - Cmaj7
Features: Three key areas (Ab, C, Eb). Bridge modulates to G major.
Why study it: Covers the most common ii-V-I patterns across multiple keys.

## There Will Never Be Another You
Key: Eb major. Form: ABAC (32 bars).
Features: Extensive use of secondary dominants. Good for substitution study.

## Stella by Starlight
Key: Bb major. Form: ABAC (32 bars).
Opens on an unusual Em7b5 - A7 (ii-V in D minor) before resolving elsewhere.
Features: Unexpected key center movements. Advanced harmonic vocabulary.
